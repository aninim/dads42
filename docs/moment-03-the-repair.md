# Moment 3: The Repair

## The Scene
A dad loses his cool — reacts too hard, shuts down, gets it wrong. The moment is real and messy. Then — five minutes later, an hour later — he comes back. Looks his kid in the eye. Says the equivalent of: *I wasn't okay. That wasn't about you. I'm working on it.*

## The Choice
The dad chooses vulnerability over pride. He doesn't pretend it didn't happen. He doesn't justify. He returns, owns it, and shows his kid that rupture isn't the end — repair is always possible.

## What the Kid Gains
*Adults aren't perfect. Growth is real. My dad is building himself, just like I'm building myself.*

That teaches a kid resilience and self-accountability before he even has words for it. It rewires what a relationship actually is — not flawless, but *repairable*. That's stronger than perfect.

## The Illustration
A father sitting with his child after a hard moment. The tension has passed. The dad's posture is open, maybe slightly leaning forward — not defensive, not performing. The kid's body language shows cautious trust returning. The air between them is quiet. Real. Healing.

## The Dads'42 Thread
The win isn't never messing up. The win is coming back. A kid who watches his father repair learns that love isn't fragile — it's built through honesty, not avoidance.
