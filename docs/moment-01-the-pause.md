# Moment 1: The Pause

## The Scene
A father stops mid-motion — bag in hand, keys visible, the shape of *going somewhere*. His child reaches for him. He kneels. Eye-to-eye. Five minutes. The world stops.

## The Choice
The dad chooses to let the urgency dissolve. The deadline, the errand, the adult machinery — it all waits. Adults can withstand a five-minute delay. Nothing breaks.

## What the Kid Gains
*I am worth stopping for. My dad chooses me.*

That builds a kid's sense of his own worth. Not codependency — real confidence that he matters. The felt sense that his dad will pause the world for him. That's a template for what love looks like when it shows up.

## The Illustration
A father mid-motion — bag, keys, the urgency visible — *stopping*. Meeting his child eye-to-eye. The moment of choice. Simple. Warm. No chaos in the frame. Just two people, eye-to-eye, and the understanding that *this* is what matters.

## The Dads'42 Thread
Every father's five minutes is different. The form doesn't matter. The intentionality does. The inward look: *What do I want my kids to know about me? About themselves?*
