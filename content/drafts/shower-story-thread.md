# Shower story
>> Final Version
  1/
    Hours have passed, but I’m still there - in that moment.
    Not to be dramatic, but it felt wer'e standing on a cliff’s edge, staring into the emotional abyss.
    One wrong step, and we’d be tumbling into a tantrum.
    ---
    2/
    Tomi, my five-year-old, didn’t want to take a shower.
    A simple request. A daily routine. Nothing new.
    But somehow, this time, it hit different.
    ---
    3/
    It was the end of a long day.
    We were both tired,
    both distracted.
    both holding the weight of invisible things.
    I tried everything — talked, guided, offered help, asked gently, even demanded.
    Nada.
    Each move pushed us further apart.
    Our patience drained quickly.
    ---
    4/
    Impatience is sneaky.
    It disguises itself as urgency.
    But often, it's fear in disguise —
    Fear that we’re losing control.
    Fear that we’re losing connection.
    In fatherhood, impatience is the fork in the road.
    I almost chose the wrong path.
    ---
    5/
    Tomi was distant and upset - I could hear it in his voice.
    He wasn't just saying no
    He was slipping into that space where my words don’t land, and my touch feels cold.
    I knew where this was heading.
    But this time, I paused.
    Not today. Not again.
    I know we can turn this around.
    We’ve done it before.
    ---
    6/
    Still, my inner voice — the one that usually guides me as a father — went quiet.
    I wasn’t sure what to do next.
    And that scared me.
    I had to do something.
    I couldn’t let this slip.
    I wanted my kid back.
    So I stopped.
    Took a breath.
    ---
    7/
    And I asked myself:
    What is *he* feeling right now?
    It wasn’t about the shower — not really.
    It was about wanting something else.
    About wanting something ***with me***.
    Inside his space. His story.
    And maybe… a plan in his mind that I’d disrupted.
    ---
    8/
    I mentalized.
    I tried to see through his eyes.
    Felt what he might be feeling.
    And then, I took a different action:
    Dropped the script.
    No words.
    No expectations.
    No commands.
    Just an open hand.
    Just me.
    ---
    9/
    Tomi reached back.
    It wasn’t too late.
    From there, everything softened.
    I don’t remember all the details —just that we ended up on the bathroom floor.
    Talking. Hugging. Holding each other.
    And yeah… he took the shower.
    10/
    I’m proud of him.
    Proud of us.
    We were on that cliff — but we didn’t fall.
    We stepped back together.
    Because that’s what fatherhood is.
    11/
    It’s our job to mentalize the emotions of our loved ones —To feel what they might be feeling, even when they can’t say it.
    It’s our job to steer their conscience gently when they’re confused or overwhelmed.
    To slow down when we feel the pull to rush in.
    12/
    And it’s our job to stay ready
    Not just to react…
    But to engage with intention and love.
    Even when we’re tired.
    ***Especially ***when we’re tired.
    ---
    13/
    We thought we were falling.
    But really, we were just looking for a softer place to land.
    And we found it — together.
    🧡
    ---
    14/
    Fellow dads, caregivers, mentors:
    Next time impatience rises…Before you fix, ask:
    ***What are they feeling right now?***
    That pause might not fix the moment —but it might ***save***** **it.
    And that’s where connection begins.
