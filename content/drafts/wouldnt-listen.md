**Problem**
I remember earlier on with my no.1. I would call his name to talk with him, and more often than not, he just wouldn't reply verbally or, heck, even acknowledge that I was addressing him.
It's not a pleasant feeling to be ignored, and surely that got me a little frustrated, which grew with time.
I'd imagine him either suddenly going deaf, which we later ruled out after a visit to a "Human experiment Lab," or him being a tiny Yoda master of selective hearing, silently registering and judging my every word to reach an informed decision to:…well, ignore me.
With time, I started thinking about it more and more, The frustration was now real, mainly because our one-on-one time was limited, as it is for most dads juggling work, life, and the occasional existential crisis, in some way or another.
I felt like I was missing out on those precious opportunities to connect with my son and actually, you know, *talk* **with **him.
**Pain**
It bothered me so much I felt I couldn't do Nothing!
**Solution**
One ordinary day while I was fixing dinner, with only me and Tomi at home, I turned over to the living area and called Tomi mentioning that dinner is on, and asking if he's hungry yet. This was one of the times he was again… silent in his world.
I mean I would be happy just getting a 'Humm'.
Then out of nowhere, really. I casually tossed out into the room air the combination of words: "Chocolate" followed by "Ice cream".
Wanna take a guess of what happened next?
It was like a switch flipped! The kid went from zero to sixty in a nanosecond, ears perked up like a Suricata on Red Bull.
I wasn't sure yet if it was a good thing or not, I was also very concern of what I just openly suggested to the audience in the room, with no means of fulfilling any such treat.
I guess we were both caught off guard…staring at each others eyes tilting between believe to disbelieve of what was just said.
On Tomi's end, this is an opportunity his not sure how and if to capitalise on or at least squize a promise to be fulfilled later.
Me on the other hand was thinking what does this selective hearing means, is it the topic, my selection of words?, is it my tone, or how I physically approach him?
Well naturally, it is all of the above and more, much more.
So I did what I was doing on my day job, which was to observe, speculate some ideas or solutions and then looking for answers to questions that came to mind in the process.
I would then try different unique actions and approaches every chance I had, and by that I mean every time the immediate surrounding was clear of distractions.
It happened very quickly, every new interaction brought wealth of opportunities, new topics that are actually of both of our interests.
**Resolution**
That "small" thing I was trying to crack, on its own taught me so much about my kid and not just how to get more of his attention when me or others ask for it.
But how his forming character behave, how he think, what draw his attention, what keeps him "wired in" to his thoughts or plays, what captivate him at this stage of his acquaintance with the world.
It is purely an aperture to his mind and soul, to his individual cosmic shine.
These moments are up for grabs, and if we will take the actions they will form the small bridges we need on this journey of ours.

    # Observe, Learn, Take Unique Actions - Repeat
    ## The Challenge
    I remember when my son wouldn't respond when I called his name. More often than not, he'd remain silent or show no sign that I was addressing him. Being ignored isn't pleasant, and my frustration grew with each passing day.
    At first, I wondered if he might be having hearing issues – which we later ruled out after a visit to the pediatrician. Then I imagined him as a tiny Yoda master of selective hearing, silently registering and judging every word before deciding to ignore me. This bothered me deeply, especially since our one-on-one time was already limited, as it is for most parents juggling work, life, and the occasional existential crisis.
    I felt like I was missing precious opportunities to connect with my son and actually *talk* **with** him.
    ## The Turning Point
    One ordinary evening while preparing dinner, with just my son Tomi and me at home, I called out to ask if he was hungry. As usual, he remained absorbed in his world. I would have been happy with even a simple "hmm" in response.
    Then, almost unconsciously, I spoke two magical words into the room: "Chocolate ice cream."
    The transformation was instantaneous. Tomi went from complete absorption to full attention in a nanosecond, his ears perking up like a meerkat on alert. We both froze, caught in a moment of mutual surprise – him wondering if this unexpected treat might materialize, me realizing I'd stumbled onto something significant.
    ## The Discovery Process
    This moment sparked my curiosity. Was it just the topic that caught his attention? My word choice? My tone of voice? Or perhaps how I physically approached him? I realized it was likely all these factors and more.
    I decided to apply my professional problem-solving approach to this parental challenge:
    1. Observe carefully
    2. Formulate potential solutions
    3. Test different approaches
    4. Learn from the results
    I began experimenting with different interactions whenever the environment was free from distractions. Each new conversation revealed opportunities to explore topics that genuinely interested both of us.
    ## The Revelation
    What started as an attempt to improve communication became a window into my son's developing personality. Through this process, I learned:
    - How his mind processes information
    - What captures and holds his attention
    - The subjects that truly engage him
    - How he interacts with the world around him
    These insights offered more than just better communication strategies – they provided glimpses into his individual spirit, his unique way of seeing the world.
    ## Moving Forward
    These moments of connection are available to all parents, waiting to be discovered through patient observation and thoughtful action. By taking time to understand our children's individual communication styles, we build the small bridges that strengthen our relationships. The process is simple but powerful: observe, learn, take unique actions, and repeat.
    The key isn't just getting our children's attention – it's understanding how they think and what matters to them. These small discoveries create pathways to deeper connections, one interaction at a time.
